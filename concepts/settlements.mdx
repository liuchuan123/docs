**Settlements** are how PayMatrix moves your collected funds to your bank account. At the end of each business day, PayMatrix batches all of your successful payments from that period, deducts the applicable processing fees, and initiates a bank transfer for the net amount. You do not need to take any action to trigger a settlement — the process runs automatically for every active merchant account on a daily schedule.

## How Settlement Amounts Are Calculated

Your settlement **net amount** is always derived from the same formula:

```
Net Amount = Gross Payment Amounts − PayMatrix Fees
```

- **Gross amount** — the sum of all successfully captured payment amounts in the settlement period, before any deductions.
- **Fee amount** — PayMatrix's processing fees for the period. Fees vary by payment method and your account's pricing plan. You can review your fee schedule in the Dashboard under **Settings → Billing**.
- **Net amount** — the exact amount that PayMatrix transfers to your bank account.

<Tip>
  Your Dashboard's **Settlements** section shows a full fee breakdown for every settlement period, including a per-payment fee itemisation you can download as a CSV for accounting purposes.
</Tip>

## Settlement Schedule

PayMatrix settles funds on a rolling daily basis. The number of business days between a payment being captured and the funds arriving in your bank account depends on your account tier.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Standard (T+2)">
    Payments captured on Day 0 are batched at end of Day 0 and arrive in your bank account **2 business days later**. This is the default schedule for new accounts.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Growth (T+1)">
    Payments captured on Day 0 arrive in your bank account the **next business day**. T+1 settlement is available to accounts on the Growth plan or above.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  Settlement timing counts **business days only** — weekends and public bank holidays do not count toward the T+1 or T+2 window. A payment captured on a Friday on a T+1 account will typically arrive the following Monday.
</Note>

## The Settlement Object

When PayMatrix creates a settlement batch, it generates a Settlement object that you can retrieve via the API or view in the Dashboard. The object captures the complete financial picture for that payout.

```json
{
  "id": "set_XYZ789",
  "status": "completed",
  "gross_amount": 150000,
  "fee_amount": 4500,
  "net_amount": 145500,
  "currency": "USD",
  "payment_count": 30,
  "period_start": "2024-01-14T00:00:00Z",
  "period_end": "2024-01-14T23:59:59Z",
  "settled_at": "2024-01-15T09:00:00Z"
}
```

<Accordion title="Settlement object field reference">

| Field | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `id` | string | Unique identifier for the settlement, prefixed with `set_`. |
| `status` | string | Current state of the settlement. See [Settlement Statuses](#settlement-statuses) below. |
| `gross_amount` | integer | Total captured payment amounts in the period, in the smallest currency unit. |
| `fee_amount` | integer | Total PayMatrix fees deducted from the gross amount. |
| `net_amount` | integer | The amount transferred to your bank account (`gross_amount − fee_amount`). |
| `currency` | string | Three-letter ISO 4217 currency code matching your settlement currency. |
| `payment_count` | integer | The number of individual payments included in this settlement batch. |
| `period_start` | string | ISO 8601 timestamp for the start of the settlement period. |
| `period_end` | string | ISO 8601 timestamp for the end of the settlement period. |
| `settled_at` | string \| null | ISO 8601 timestamp of when the bank transfer was completed. `null` until the transfer clears. |

</Accordion>

## Settlement Statuses

A settlement progresses through the following states from batch creation to bank transfer completion.

| Status | Description |
|---|---|
| `pending` | The settlement batch has been created at end-of-day. PayMatrix is preparing the payout details and confirming the net amount. |
| `processing` | The bank transfer has been initiated. Funds are in transit to your bank account. |
| `completed` | The transfer has cleared and funds are available in your bank account. The `settled_at` timestamp is populated. |
| `failed` | The bank transfer was rejected, typically due to invalid account details or a bank-side error. PayMatrix will retry on the next business day and notify you by email. |

<Warning>
  If a settlement reaches `failed` status, check that your payout bank account details in **Settings → Payouts** are correct. Repeated failures may temporarily pause your settlement schedule until the issue is resolved.
</Warning>

<Note>
  If a settlement remains in `processing` status beyond **T+3 business days**, please contact PayMatrix Support at [support@paymatrix.io](mailto:support@paymatrix.io) with your settlement ID. Our team can investigate the transfer status directly with the receiving bank.
</Note>

## Settlement Reports

Each completed settlement includes a downloadable report you can use for bookkeeping and reconciliation. Reports are available in CSV format and include one row per payment with the gross amount, fee, and net contribution of each transaction.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Settlements tab">
    In the PayMatrix Dashboard, navigate to **Settlements** in the left sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a settlement period">
    Click on any completed settlement to open its detail view, showing the gross amount, fee breakdown, and list of included payments.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download the CSV report">
    Click **Download Report** to export the full payment-level breakdown as a CSV file. This file is compatible with most accounting tools including QuickBooks and Xero.
  </Step>
</Steps>
